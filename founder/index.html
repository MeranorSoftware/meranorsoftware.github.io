Meet our Founder

Josh Welch

Builder. Designer. Problem-solver. Curious by nature.

Meranor began with a simple belief: if something can help people, teach people, or make life a little more meaningful, it is worth building.

Josh created Meranor as a home for thoughtful software, AI, and technology projects — tools built from curiosity, refined through real use, and shaped by the belief that ordinary people can build extraordinary things when they keep learning.

“I want Meranor to prove that you do not have to wait for permission to build something meaningful. You can start with curiosity, learn as you go, and build what matters.”

Why Meranor Exists

To turn curiosity into useful, thoughtful technology.

Meranor is built around a hands-on philosophy: follow the questions that matter, make something real, learn from it, and keep improving. FieldKeeper, Astra AI, Geomancer, PatchCube, and CountLens all come from that same place — the desire to build tools that help people observe more, create more, and understand more.

The Builder’s Rule

Build slow enough to care. Build bold enough to matter.

The goal is not to chase every trend or make software louder. The goal is to make tools that feel intentional, useful, and worth the attention they ask for.
